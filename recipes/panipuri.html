Pani Puri

Description

Pani Puri, also known as Golgappa or Puchka, is a popular street food snack in India. It consists of hollow, crispy puris filled with spicy, tangy water, mashed potatoes, and chickpeas.

Ingredients

  • Puri (hollow crispy shells)
  • Spicy and tangy water (pani)
  • Mashed potatoes
  • Boiled chickpeas
  • Tamarind chutney
  • Mint chutney
  • Chaat masala
  • Finely chopped onions (optional)
  • Finely chopped coriander leaves (optional)
  • Sev (thin gram flour noodles, optional)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Puris:
    • Make a stiff dough using semolina (sooji) and all-purpose flour (maida).
    • Roll out small portions of the dough into thin circles and deep fry them until they puff up and turn golden brown.
    • Drain excess oil and store the puris in an airtight container.
  2. Prepare the Fillings:
    • Mash the boiled potatoes and mix them with chaat masala and salt.
    • Boil and season the chickpeas with salt and chaat masala.
  3. Assemble the Pani Puri:
    • Make a small hole in each puri.
    • Stuff them with mashed potatoes, boiled chickpeas, chopped onions, and sev.
    • Dip each stuffed puri into spicy tangy water (pani).
    • Optionally, garnish with tamarind chutney and mint chutney.
