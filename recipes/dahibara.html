Dahi-Bara

Description

Dahibara Aludum is a variant of Dahi vada and originated from Cuttack of the state Odisha. It is a type of chaat (snack) from Cuttack and is popular throughout India.

Ingredients

  • Vada
  • dahi (yogurt)
  • Potato Curry
  • Pea Curry

  • Steps

    1. Soak and Grind the Lentils:
      • Wash and soak urad dal in water for about 4-6 hours or overnight.
      • Drain the water and grind the dal into a smooth paste using minimal water. The batter should be thick and fluffy.
    2. Seasoning the Batter:
      • Add salt to the batter and mix well. You can also add grated ginger to enhance the flavor.
    3. Frying the Dumplings:
      • Heat oil in a deep frying pan.
      • Wet your hands and take a small portion of the batter, shape it into small round dumplings, and gently drop them into the hot oil.
      • Fry until they turn golden brown and crispy from all sides.
      • Once fried, remove them from the oil and drain excess oil on a paper towel.
    4. Preparation of Yogurt Mixture:
      • Take beaten yogurt in a bowl.
      • Add salt, cumin powder, chaat masala, red chili powder, chopped green chilies, and coriander leaves. Mix well to make a smooth yogurt mixture.
    5. Soaking the Dumplings:
      • Once the dumplings are fried and slightly cooled, soak them in lukewarm water for about 15-20 minutes. This step helps in making the dumplings soft.
    6. Assembling:
      • Squeeze out excess water from the soaked dumplings and arrange them in a serving dish.
      • Pour the prepared yogurt mixture generously over the dumplings.
    7. Tempering (Optional):
      • Heat oil in a small pan, add mustard seeds.
      • Once the mustard seeds start spluttering, add a pinch of asafoetida.
      • Pour this tempering over the yogurt-covered dumplings.
    8. Chilling (Optional):
      • You can refrigerate the Dahibara for some time before serving to enhance the flavor and chilliness.
    9. Serving:
      • Serve the Dahibara garnished with some more chaat masala, red chili powder, and chopped coriander leaves.
      • It can be enjoyed as a snack or a light meal.
